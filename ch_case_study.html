Clipboard Health Case Study
Analyzing Centers for Medicaid and Medicare Long-Term Staffing Data

The assignment involved providing strategic recommendations for Clipboard Health’s Sales Leadership team based on insights from the Q2 2024 CMS Payroll-Based Journal Nurse Staffing report, published by the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS). An initial analysis of the data showed no immediately obvious patterns or trends. However, I identified opportunities by focusing on targeting facilities with specific staffing needs and compliance gaps.

The first recommendation was to target facilities that reported zero contractor hours, suggesting they may benefit from contractor services. I refined this approach by prioritizing facilities with lower-than-average hours per resident per day (HPRD) in total nursing staff, as well as those with below-average CMS ratings. Facilities meeting these criteria were visualized on an interactive geographic bubble plot, which displayed facility name, location, and relevant statistics in hover-boxes.

The second recommendation targeted facilities with available beds that could be utilized with a few additional contractor shifts per day. Facilities requiring fewer than one or more than six shifts to fill all open beds were excluded. Three separate geographic bubble plots visualized these results by total contractor shifts needed, registered nurse (RN) shifts needed, and certified nursing assistant (CNA) shifts needed. Color-coding and bubble size indicated the number of shifts required, adding clarity to the visualization.

The final recommendation focused on facilities that would be non-compliant if CMS’s new HPRD requirements, outlined in April 2024, were implemented immediately. These new rules mandate specific HPRD for RNs, CNAs, total licensed staff, and require 24/7 on-site RN coverage. Facilities that would fall short in any of these areas were plotted on a geographic bubble plot, with bubble color and size indicating the number of potential compliance deficiencies under the new rules.

To present these insights, I chose HTML as the format, allowing for interactive visualizations using Plotly. This approach provided a more dynamic presentation compared to the typical static ouput of a PDF document.
